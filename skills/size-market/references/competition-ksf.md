# Competition and KSF

## Competition map

Classify competitors by the customer job they solve, not only by product labels:

- Direct category products.
- Adjacent suites or platforms bundling the feature.
- Workflow substitutes, including labor and agencies.
- Free/open-source alternatives.
- Upstream or downstream owners able to integrate into the category.

Include the modeled company itself. For each row record target segment, geography, monetization, distribution, scale indicator, modeled relevant revenue, revenue share of TAM, source IDs, and strategic advantage. Link modeled revenue and share to the revenue and reconciliation sheets. Leave unknown numeric fields blank rather than replacing them with qualitative share labels.

## Structural analysis

Assess:

- Buyer concentration and willingness to pay.
- Supplier, platform, app-store, cloud, data, or model dependency.
- Distribution and switching costs.
- Network effects, data advantages, workflow lock-in, brand, and ecosystem.
- Economies of scale and unit-cost curves.
- Bundling and price compression.
- Regulation, rights, privacy, security, and trust.
- Where gross profit and bargaining power accrue across the value chain.

Distinguish current share from durable power. Traffic, downloads, users, revenue, and enterprise seats measure different things.

## Key success factors

A KSF must connect a capability to an economic outcome. Use the pattern:

`Capability → operating metric → economic effect → evidence`

Examples:

- Distribution embedded in a high-frequency workflow → lower CAC and higher activation → faster paid-user growth.
- Proprietary data or feedback loops → better quality/retention → higher conversion and pricing power.
- Low unit cost and latency → broader viable use cases → higher usage at acceptable gross margin.
- Enterprise controls and rights assurance → shorter risk review and larger contracts → access to high-ARPPU segments.

Separate:

- Table stakes: required to compete but unlikely to create durable advantage.
- Differentiators: drivers of win rate, retention, pricing, or cost.
- Bottleneck KSFs: scarce capabilities controlling the next stage of market growth.

## Required conclusion

Write five concise sections in `11_竞争KSF`:

1. Market structure today.
2. How demand and the value chain are changing.
3. Where competition is intensifying or commoditizing.
4. The three to five most important KSFs.
5. Leading indicators and facts that would falsify the thesis.

Tie every material claim to source IDs or mark it as an analysis judgment. Avoid unexplained labels such as `Analyst` or `Analyst bridge`.
